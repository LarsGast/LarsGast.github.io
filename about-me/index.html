About Me

Introduction

Hi there! My name is Lars. On this website I document my journey to becoming a better developer.

I currently work as a Junior Software Developer in The Netherlands.

School

In 2018 I started my studies in Applied Mathematics, which I finished in 2022. Applied Mathematics isn't a software development degree. In fact, I only remember about 3 or 4 subjects regarding programming in Python and R. Still, I spent my internship and graduation period working for the company that currently employs me as a junior software developer.

During my studies in Applied Mathematics, I learned about a lot of different mathematical concepts, of which programming is one. In the end, it's less about learning the ins and outs of these specific concepts, and more about being able to apply my knowledge to real world problems. I don't have to know exactly how everything works, as long as I know that problems can sometimes be translated into math, be solved, and then be translated back into common language. I learned logical thinking and problem solving, concepts that are very useful in software development.

Career

After graduating, the company that I spent my internship and graduation with offered me a job as a Junior Developer, and I still hold that position today.

At this job, I learned lots about programming and software development, as my time at school was more focused on general logical thinking and problem solving. I quickly picked up on C#, TypeScript, and SQL, which are the languages that the company uses to run their website. I spent most of my time in the backend with C# and SQL, but I also dabbled in TypeScript and React for frontend work. Backend was definitely my favorite domain, but I knew frontend and database well enough to start calling myself a full-stack developer.

In 2024, I took an interest in broadening my knowledge with pieces of information that I could have learned if I had picked a more software-focused degree. I don't regret my decision in choosing Applied Mathematics as a major, but I had noticed there were some gaps in my knowledge regarding certain software development concepts and terms. If anything, my degree in Applied Mathematics helped me in a way that would allow me to learn more efficiently.

Hobby projects

Next to school and work, I also like to work on my own personal hobby projects. Whenever I work with some new thing at my job about which I want learn more, I'll set up a repository in my free time to play with. This website is an example of such a personal project, as I wanted to learn more about HTML and CSS.

You can find more information about my personal project on the Projects page, which you can find in the navigation bar at the top of each page.
